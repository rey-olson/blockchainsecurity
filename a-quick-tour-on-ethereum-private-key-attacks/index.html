Background

When I started this blog I originally wanted to zoom in on the entire spectrum of possible attacks on blockchains and the corresponding defenses, because the blockchain sphere is just so vast.

Just like everywhere else, you need to sufficiently secure the whole eco-system, with all its (moving) parts. And so it becomes quickly, quite complex.

It's not only 'blockchain security' (or backend security). Or your run-off-the-mill frontend web security. Or application security in general. Or anti-malware software on endpoints for that matter. No, it's all of these and much more: it's the sum of all parts.

Zero risk or one hundred percent security is a myth

Think your money is safe in a DeFi smart contract containing millions of dolllars/euros/ether worth of value? Don't forget the (often anonymous) dev team behind the scenes, that wield the keys to these contracts (i.e. the keys to the kingdom). If they make a mistake, then these contracts get drained and money is lost.

It's a bit disheartening to see, but over the last year, the main culprit of many blockchain related 'hacks' was not smart contract exploits, but plain old phishing. The high-profile smart contract exploits have all but disappeared, but cyber criminals focus on low-effort-high return types of attacks.
